The problem

Some work should never be sent to the cloud.

Your team may want AI assistance for confidential analysis, R&D files, client documents, legal material, internal reports, or sensitive multilingual work. But the most valuable documents are often the ones that cannot be casually uploaded to public AI tools or third-party systems.

For these workflows, the question is not only which model is best. The question is whether the information stays inside a boundary your team can actually control.

Approved documents stay inside the machine boundary. Work can be processed locally without requiring an external model provider for the core workflow.
Access is limited to authorized users and whatever policy scope you configure for the deployment, whether that is by session, user, project, or secure room.
The system can be configured with encrypted local storage, controlled access, and scoped or resettable memory so the device does not simply become a readable archive if control is lost.
Yes. Approved local document workflows can run offline, and connected or hybrid behavior can remain optional rather than required.
Smart Fellow Sovereign is built for that environment.
What it is

A dedicated AI machine inside your control.

Smart Fellow Sovereign is an offline AI workstation deployed for a specific team, project, or confidential workflow.

It has its own compute, local AI capabilities, encrypted storage, and controlled memory. It does not need internet access to process approved documents. It does not automatically connect to company systems. It only works with what your team explicitly gives it.

Why not a normal AI tool?

Public AI tools are built for access. This is built for containment.

Most AI products are designed around connectivity: cloud models, online search, integrations, synchronization, and remote access. That is useful for many workflows.

But for highly confidential work, connectivity can become the risk. Smart Fellow Sovereign is designed around the opposite principle: containment.

If the machine is stolen

Designed for the moment things go wrong.

Confidential systems should not only work when everything goes perfectly. They should be designed for accidents, misuse, and loss of control.

Smart Fellow Sovereign can be configured so that confidential data is encrypted locally, access requires authorized credentials, and memory can be scoped or wiped according to policy.

If the device is lost or stolen, the goal is simple: the machine should not expose usable company information to an unauthorized person.

Memory under control

Memory is useful. That is why it must be controlled.

AI memory is powerful because it lets the Fellow understand your terminology, writing style, documents, previous decisions, and workflow. But memory is also sensitive.

It remembers only what it is allowed to remember, for as long as it is allowed to remember it.

What this is not

Not every workflow needs this.

Smart Fellow Sovereign is for high-sensitivity work. If your team needs live market monitoring, external research, connected tools, or internet-based workflows, a hybrid Smart Fellow deployment may be a better fit.

Offline mode is for situations where confidentiality, containment, and local control matter more than live connectivity.
